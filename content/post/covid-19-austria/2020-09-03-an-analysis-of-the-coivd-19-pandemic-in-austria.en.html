The World Health Organization (WHO) declared the COVID-19 outbreak as a pandemic on March 11, 2020 (“WHO Director-General’s Opening Remarks at the Media Briefing on Covid-19 - 11 March 2020,” n.d.). COVID-19 first reported in December 2019 in Wuhan China, is a respiratory disease caused by a new form of the coronavirus. With no confirmed treatment, and the rate of spread across the world, COVID-19 has impacted almost all countries across the world, with Austria being no exception.

Austria saw growing daily coronavirus (COVID-19) case numbers, especially since March 2020. Although Austria is no longer among the countries that are most affected by the virus or those that reported a large number of deaths, it would be interesting to analyse the spread of COVID-19 in Austria over time.

Data Description

The database of daily COVID-19 cases, deaths and recoveries in the countries belonging to the European Union Civil Protection Mechanism (UCPM) maintained by the European Commission Joint Research Centre (JRC) is used within the analysis of the COVID-19 pandemic in Austria (The European Commission Joint Research Centre (JRC) n.d.). An overview of COVID-19 in Austria

In gaining an insight into the spread of COVID-19 in Austria, figure 1 plots the trend of the cumulative counts of total confirmed COVID-19 cases, deaths and recoveries in Austria.

Figure 1: The trend of cumulative COIVD cases, deaths and recoveries in Austria

The trend in COVID-19 since early 2020 up to around March is quite a sudden increase, thereafter the rate of increase suddenly plateaus in around May. The rate of increase is much lower from May to July and thereafter increases again. This may be an effect of the social distancing restrictions being eased in Austria. The number of recoveries isn’t recorded in the initial few months of 2020 and suddenly reports a large number in mid April. This might be due to a collective number of recoveries from the novel coronavirus being confirmed. Thereafter, it’s interesting how the number of recoveries follow a similar trend to that of the confirmed cases, but lower in number. The number of deaths, starting in around end April increases at a very low but steady rate.

The highest number of COVID-19 patients, deaths and recoveries has been reported in Wien while Burgernlad reports the lowest number of cases, deaths and recoveries. Although Wien reports the highest number of deaths in Austria, Steiermark reports a massive 5.72% of deaths of the COVID-19 cases in the state and also has a comparatively low percentage of recoveries at 84.23%. Vorarlberg reports a lower number of cases and deaths as well as a lower proportion of deaths to cases, and also has a high percentage of recoveries at 92.59%. Further, Wien reports the lowest percentage of recoveries while Oberösterreich reports the lowest percentage of deaths.

Figure 2 show the trends in the cumulative number of COVID-19 cases across the 9 states in Austria. The trends in all states except for Oberösterreich and Wien behave quite similarly, although at different absolute numbers; starting off in early March, rapidly increases till mid April and thereafter almost plateaus with a very small rate of increase till mid July and starts to increase again gradually. The trend of COVID-19 cases in Oberösterreich follows a similar pattern till early July, after which the counts increase rapidly. The trend of cases in Wien behave completely different to the rest of the states; starting at almost the same time as the others increases at almost a steady rate till end July and starts to increase rapidly thereafter.
